# In-Kind Donations - User Manual

## Overview
The In-Kind Donation page allows you to record non-cash donations such as equipment, tools, supplies, or services donated to the church.

**Location**: Transactions ‚Üí In-Kind Donation  
**Permissions**: Admin, Bookkeeper  
**Page URL**: `/transactions/in-kind`

---

## What Are In-Kind Donations?

**In-Kind donations** are contributions of goods or services instead of cash, such as:
- Equipment (riding mower, computers, furniture)
- Supplies (office supplies, food, materials)
- Services (legal work, construction, professional services)
- Property (land, buildings - requires special handling)

**Important IRS Rules:**
- The CHURCH does NOT assign a value to in-kind donations
- The DONOR is responsible for determining fair market value
- Items over $5,000 may require independent appraisal
- Church provides acknowledgment of receipt, not valuation

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. Navigate to In-Kind Page
- Click **Transactions** in top navigation
- Select **In-Kind Donation**
- OR go to `/transactions/in-kind`

### 2. Select Date
- **Date field**: Date item was received
- Use the date the donation was accepted by the church

### 3. Select Donor (REQUIRED)
**Why Required?** IRS requires donor information for tax-deductible in-kind donations.

- **Search existing donor**: Type name or envelope number
- **Add new donor**: Click "+ Add New Donor" if not in list
- Cannot proceed without selecting a donor

### 4. Enter Item Description
- **Item Description field**: Describe what was donated
- Be specific and detailed
- Examples:
  - "John Deere X300 Riding Lawn Mower"
  - "Dell Optiplex 7010 Desktop Computer"
  - "50 boxes of copy paper"
  - "Professional landscaping services"

**Best Practice**: Include brand, model, and condition when applicable.

### 5. Enter Estimated Value
- **Estimated Value field**: Dollar amount
- This is the donor's stated fair market value
- Must be greater than $0.01

**Important Disclaimers:**
- Value is PROVIDED BY DONOR
- Church does not determine value
- Church only acknowledges receipt
- Donor responsible for documentation
- Items over $5,000 may require appraisal

### 6. Choose Category
Two options:

#### Option A: Fixed Asset (1000s)
**Use for**: Long-term equipment the church will use

Examples:
- Riding lawn mower
- Desktop computers
- Office furniture
- Musical instruments
- Sound equipment
- Vehicles

**Accounting**: Item goes on Balance Sheet as an Asset

#### Option B: Donated Supply/Expense (5000s)
**Use for**: Items that will be consumed or used immediately

Examples:
- Office supplies (paper, pens)
- Food for events
- Cleaning supplies
- Materials for VBS
- Disposable items

**Accounting**: Item recorded as an Expense immediately

**How to Choose:**
- Will you use it for more than one year? ‚Üí Fixed Asset
- Will it be consumed/used up soon? ‚Üí Donated Supply

### 7. Select Account
Account dropdown changes based on category:

#### If Fixed Asset Selected:
Choose from Asset accounts (1000-1999):
- 1400 - Equipment and Furniture
- 1500 - Vehicles
- 1600 - Musical Instruments
- etc.

#### If Donated Supply Selected:
Choose from Expense accounts (5000-5999):
- 5400 - Office Expenses
- 5300 - Ministry Expenses
- 5200 - Facilities Expenses
- etc.

### 8. Select Fund
Choose which fund receives the donation:
- **General Fund** - Unrestricted donations
- **Building Fund** - Building-related items
- **Mission Fund** - Mission-related items
- etc.

### 9. Add Reference Number (Optional)
- Receipt number
- Donation tracking number
- Any internal reference

### 10. Review and Submit
Check the blue information box showing:
- Debit: Selected asset or expense account
- Credit: 4050 - Non-Cash Contributions
- Note: NO checking account involved

Click **"Record In-Kind Donation"**

## Accounting Logic

### Fixed Asset In-Kind Donation
```
Example: $3,500 riding mower donated

Journal Entry:
- Debit: 1400 - Equipment (Asset increases)
- Credit: 4050 - Non-Cash Contributions (Income increases)

Result: 
- Asset goes on Balance Sheet
- Income recognized on Income Statement
- NO cash involved
```

### Donated Supply In-Kind Donation
```
Example: $250 office supplies donated

Journal Entry:
- Debit: 5400 - Office Expenses (Expense increases)
- Credit: 4050 - Non-Cash Contributions (Income increases)

Result:
- Expense and Income both increase
- Net Income unchanged (expense = income)
- NO cash involved
```

## Donor Statement Impact

In-kind donations appear on donor statements but are handled differently than cash:

**On Statement:**
- Listed in separate "In-Kind (Non-Cash) Contributions" section
- Shows date and description
- NO dollar amount shown on statement
- Blue badge marks as "In-Kind"

**IRS Disclaimer Included:**
- Donor responsible for determining value
- Church provides acknowledgment only
- Church does not assign valuation
- Donor responsible for documentation

## Common Questions

### Q: Can the church tell the donor what their donation is worth?
**A**: NO. The church cannot and should not assign a value. This is the donor's responsibility.

### Q: What if the donor asks us to value their donation?
**A**: Politely explain:
- IRS rules prohibit us from assigning value
- Donor must determine fair market value
- Donor should consult tax professional
- Items over $5,000 may need professional appraisal

### Q: Do we record services as in-kind donations?
**A**: Generally, no. Volunteer services are not tax-deductible to the donor, so there's no need to track them as in-kind donations. However, if a professional provides services at no charge that would normally be billed, this could be recorded.

### Q: What if the item is clearly not worth what the donor says?
**A**: Record what the donor states, but:
- You can suggest they consult a tax professional
- You are not required to challenge their valuation
- Your responsibility is only acknowledgment

### Q: Should we record small in-kind donations (under $25)?
**A**: It's up to your church policy. Many churches have a minimum threshold ($25 or $50) for recording in-kind donations to reduce administrative work.

### Q: What if someone donates an old car?
**A**: Special rules apply:
- Vehicles require IRS Form 1098-C
- Church must file with IRS within 30 days
- Consult with your accountant for proper handling
- May require separate process
