# Balance Sheet - User Manual

## Overview
The Balance Sheet shows your church's financial position at a specific point in time - what you own (assets), what you owe (liabilities), and your net worth (equity).

**Location**: Reports â†’ Balance Sheet  
**Permissions**: Admin, Bookkeeper, Viewer (all users)  
**Page URL**: `/reports/balance-sheet`

---

## What is a Balance Sheet?

**Balance Sheet** = Financial snapshot showing:
- **Assets**: What you own (cash, equipment, etc.)
- **Liabilities**: What you owe (bills, loans, etc.)
- **Equity**: Net worth (Assets - Liabilities)

**Formula**: Assets = Liabilities + Equity

**Also Called**: Statement of Financial Position

## Page Components

### 1. Fund Balance Cards (Top)

Shows balance for each fund:
- **General Fund**: $X,XXX (Unrestricted)
- **Building Fund**: $X,XXX (Restricted)
- **Mission Fund**: $X,XXX (Restricted)
- etc.

**What this shows**: How your total cash is allocated across purposes.

**Total**: All fund cards add up to total net assets.

### 2. Date Selector

**As of Date field**:
- Defaults to today
- Select any past date to see historical balance
- Future dates not allowed

**Example**: Select "12/31/2025" to see year-end position

### 3. Balance Sheet Report

Three main sections:

#### ASSETS (What You Own)
- Current Assets
  - Cash accounts (checking, savings)
  - Accounts receivable
  - Prepaid expenses
- Fixed Assets
  - Equipment and furniture
  - Vehicles
  - Buildings and land
  - Less: Accumulated depreciation

#### LIABILITIES (What You Owe)
- Current Liabilities
  - Accounts payable
  - Accrued expenses
  - Short-term loans
- Long-Term Liabilities
  - Mortgages
  - Long-term loans
  - Notes payable

#### NET ASSETS (Equity)
- Unrestricted net assets
- Temporarily restricted net assets
- Permanently restricted net assets
- Current year surplus/deficit

### 4. Account Details

Each account shows:
- **Account Number and Name**: e.g., "1100 - Operating Checking"
- **Current Balance**: Dollar amount
- **Fund Breakdown**: If applicable

## Fund Balances Explained

### What Are Fund Balances?

Fund balances show how your net assets are designated:

**Unrestricted Funds**:
- Can be used for any purpose
- Board has full discretion
- Example: General Fund

**Temporarily Restricted Funds**:
- Donor-designated for specific purpose
- Must be used as designated
- Examples: Building Fund, Mission Fund

**Permanently Restricted Funds** (rare):
- Principal must be maintained
- Only earnings can be spent
- Example: Endowment Fund

### Reading Fund Cards

Each card shows:
- **Fund Name**: e.g., "General Fund"
- **Balance**: Total designated to this fund
- **Status**: (Unrestricted) or (Restricted)

**Important**: Fund cards add up to Total Net Assets!

## Common Questions

### Q: Why are fund balances different from bank account balances?
**A**: 
- **Bank accounts** (Assets) = Physical money location
- **Funds** (Equity) = Purpose designation
- One checking account can hold multiple funds

**Example**:
- Checking account: $50,000
- But that $50,000 includes:
  - $30,000 General Fund
  - $15,000 Building Fund
  - $5,000 Mission Fund

### Q: Should Total Assets equal Total Net Assets?
**A**: Only if you have zero liabilities. Formula:
```
Total Assets = Total Liabilities + Total Net Assets
```

### Q: What if Net Assets is negative?
**A**: You owe more than you own. Serious financial trouble. Immediate action needed.

### Q: Why is a Fund showing negative?
**A**: You spent more from that fund than it had. Either:
- Error in recording (check transactions)
- Need to transfer from another fund
- Temporarily overspent (move money back)

### Q: Do voided transactions appear?
**A**: No, voided transactions are automatically excluded.

### Q: Can I see previous months?
**A**: Yes! Change the "As of Date" to any past date.

### Q: Why doesn't this match my bank statement?
**A**: Balance Sheet includes ALL assets (checking, savings, equipment, etc.), not just checking. Plus accounts payable and other items.

## Troubleshooting

### Balance Sheet Doesn't Balance
**Problem**: Assets â‰  Liabilities + Equity  
**Causes**:
- Unbalanced journal entry exists
- System error
- Database corruption

**Solutions**:
- Check Transaction History for unbalanced entries
- Run journal_entry_balances query
- Contact administrator

### Fund Balance Negative
**Problem**: Fund shows negative amount  
**Solutions**:
- Review transactions for that fund
- Check if miscoded
- Transfer from another fund
- Void errors

### Numbers Seem Wrong
**Problem**: Amounts don't match expectations  
**Check**:
- Date selected (might be past date)
- All transactions recorded
- No duplicate entries
- Voided transactions properly marked

### Can't See Certain Accounts
**Problem**: Expected accounts not showing  
**Solutions**:
- Accounts with $0 balance may be hidden
- Check Chart of Accounts
- Verify account type correct
